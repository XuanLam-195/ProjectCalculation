Epics

An epic refers to a large and high-level user story or requirement that captures a significant functionality or business objective.

Requirements

Requirements refer to the documented and agreed-upon descriptions of what a system should do or the qualities it should possess. They serve as the foundation for designing, developing, and testing a system to ensure that it meets the desired objectives and satisfies stakeholder needs.

Backlog

A backlog refers to a prioritized list of all the features, user stories, bug fixes, and other work items that need to be addressed in a product or project. The backlog serves as a single source of truth for the Scrum team, providing visibility into upcoming work and guiding the development process.

Sprints

A sprint refers to a fixed timebox or iteration during which a specific amount of work is completed. It is a time-bound period, typically lasting between one to four weeks, during which the Scrum team focuses on delivering a potentially shippable increment of the product.
